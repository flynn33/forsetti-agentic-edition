# Compliance

> Canonical source: [`COMPLIANCE_POLICY.md`](../COMPLIANCE_POLICY.md)

Summary of the compliance model.

## Core Doctrine

Compliance is determined by evidence, not confidence. A change is compliant only when required policy conditions are satisfied AND required evidence is present.

## Compliance Outcomes

| Outcome | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| **Pass** | All conditions met, evidence present, no unresolved issues |
| **Request Changes** | Partially met conditions, fixable without rework |
| **Block** | Fundamental violation — false completion, unauthorized scope, protected asset violation |

## Blocking Violations

Ten blocking violations are defined (FAE-C001 through FAE-C010), covering: missing task contracts, unauthorized scope, protected asset violations, false completion claims, silent breaking changes, missing changelog entries, missing documentation, hidden failures, unsubstantiated assumptions, and contradictions between summary and evidence.
